# PEP Visual Identity — Better Social Drink

## Core idea
PEP should look like the drink you are happy to order when everyone else is choosing cocktails, beers, wines or mocktails. The system makes protein feel like a quiet bonus, not the headline. The visual language is fruity, social and premium-but-approachable, with enough bar credibility for hospitality and enough playful energy for festivals, dates and dinner plans.

## Colour system
The palette balances three needs: social warmth, adult premium depth and fruit-led refreshment. Spritz Coral is the main recognition colour; Velvet Plum provides the evening, hospitality anchor; Mango Spark adds celebratory fruit energy. Champagne Cream and Peach Glow keep layouts light and orderable, while After-Dark Grape creates a more nighttime, venue-ready mode.

Use colour in bold, simple blocks rather than busy rainbow clutter. Product facts should sit in small, confident badges rather than dominating the pack. Avoid clinical whites/blues, gym neons, black-and-metal performance styling, fake spirits heritage and kids’ juice brightness.

## Typography
PEP uses a warm, expressive display serif for flavour and occasion-led headlines, a clean modern sans for readability, a restrained script accent for serve notes, and a geometric badge face for product facts. The mix should feel like a premium social drink menu, not a supplement tub.

Display headlines can be large, rounded and charismatic. Body copy should be calm and clear. Script should be used sparingly: one short phrase at a time, never paragraphs. Badge type should be uppercase and compact for quick reading on cans, venue cards and social placements.

## Layout language
Lead with the occasion first: serve moments, flavour, arrival energy, friends, food, music, tables, golden-hour or evening atmospheres. Use protein as a discreet credibility layer. Strong compositions should include generous negative space, oversized wordmark moments, clean badges and simple fruit/spark motifs.

## Packaging principles
The can should look like something that belongs in a bar fridge, not a gym vending machine. Keep the front hierarchy simple: PEP large, tagline or flavour note second, product facts third. Use a premium wrap with coral, cream, plum and mango accents; include small sparkle/fizz motifs and fruit shapes, but avoid cartoons or dense functional claims.

## Imagery principles
Photography should feel candid but polished: outdoor tables, bar tops, festival-adjacent light, dinner moments, ice, glassware, fruit garnish and movement. Avoid visible drunkenness, hard-party mess, workout settings, medical/science props, supplement scoops or overt alcohol imitation.

## Motifs
Reusable elements include citrus wheels, mango crescents, sparkle bursts, fizz bubbles, cocktail picks and curved garnish ribbons. Keep them flat, minimal and adult. Never use leaves or botanical marks as identity shortcuts.

## Brand behaviour
PEP speaks like a confident host: light, upbeat and unforced. It should say, “you can join in,” not “you must optimise.” The identity should make alcohol-free and protein-enhanced feel naturally social rather than worthy or functional.
