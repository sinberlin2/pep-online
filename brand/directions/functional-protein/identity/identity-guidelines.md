# PEP Visual Identity — Functional Protein Direction

## Brand idea
PEP should look like a clean, premium, active protein drink for everyday routines — not a gym-bro shake, not a meal replacement, and not a vague wellness soda. The visual system is built around clear benefit communication, light refreshment, and confident functional structure.

## Colour principles
- Lead with crisp blue, navy, white, and cool refreshment tones.
- Use bright citrus-lime only as a precision accent for protein numbers, CTA moments, or key badges.
- Keep layouts open and structured; colour should guide comprehension, not decorate every surface.
- Avoid black/red supplement aggression, soft lifestyle-only pastels, cocktail/bar colours, and childlike fruit-juice palettes.

## Core palette
- Kinetic Blue `#176BFF` — the main recognition colour for wordmark, active states, and benefit emphasis.
- Protein Navy `#10243E` — the premium anchor for headlines, dark panels, and serious functional credibility.
- Fresh Mint `#4EE3B4` — a fresh active accent for motion, flavour, and refreshment.
- Citrus Pulse `#D7FF3F` — high-impact utility accent for CTAs and nutrition callouts.
- Alpine White `#F7FBFC` and Mist Blue `#EAF6FF` — the main clean background system.

## Typography
PEP uses a geometric, functional sans system with a technical badge layer and one restrained human accent.
- Display: Sora SemiBold/Bold — confident, modern, active headlines.
- Body: Inter Regular/Medium — clear product explanations, web UI, nutritional copy.
- Script accent: Caveat Medium — very small human notes only; never for core claims.
- Badge: IBM Plex Mono SemiBold — protein facts, calorie markers, utility tags, measurement-style details.

## Layout language
- Use a strong top-to-bottom hierarchy: brand, benefit, flavour, facts.
- Build labels around clean vertical bands, thin rules, and exact nutrition modules.
- Use motion arrows, dot grids, measurement ticks, ice facets, citrus wedges, and sparkle accents as reusable graphic devices.
- Keep every composition benefit-first: the protein number should be immediately findable.

## Photography and imagery
Photography should feel bright, active, and real: gym bag, cycling kit, laptop desk, fridge door, café counter, post-run bench, cold condensation, daylight, and clean surfaces. Avoid exaggerated fitness poses, shirtless body-transformation imagery, cocktail garnishes as the hero, or heavy shake textures.

## Logo direction
The mark is a simple kinetic protein symbol: three rounded capsules/dots arranged in a forward motion path, suggesting convenience, measurable nutrition, and light refreshment. It should feel precise and modern enough for functional credibility but friendly enough for daily repeat use.
