# PEP brand guidelines — Better social drink

For Lou & Shannon / pep-drink.com  
Selected direction: **3 — Better social drink**  
Tagline: **Feel good. Have fun.**

## Positioning in one sentence
PEP is a premium, light, refreshing protein drink for social and out-of-home moments: the drink you order when you want the ritual, taste, and energy of going out without defaulting to alcohol or boring soft drinks.

## Who PEP is for
- Socially active adults who care about what they drink, but do not want the evening to feel like a health routine.
- People who like bars, dinners, festivals, dates, rooftops, terraces, hotels, and events, but want a better-feeling option.
- People choosing less alcohol, no alcohol, or simply more variety without losing the feeling of the occasion.
- Aesthetic, experience-led customers who buy because the drink feels exciting, sharable, and worth ordering.
- Consumers who appreciate protein as a bonus, not as the main reason to join the brand.

## When they drink it
- Before or during dinner as a first round alternative.
- At bars when friends are having cocktails, beer, wine, or mocktails.
- At festivals, rooftop events, hotel bars, beach clubs, terraces, and dates.
- During daytime social moments where a soft drink feels too basic and alcohol feels too heavy.
- As a flavour-led treat that still feels light and refreshing.

## Core strategic idea
PEP must make protein feel socially desirable. The drink should not look or sound like a gym product entering a bar. It should feel like a social ritual first, with protein as a surprising added benefit.

## Approved personality adjectives
Use these as the stable brand filter:
- social
- fun
- premium
- refreshing
- fruity
- experiential
- aesthetic
- celebratory
- feel-good
- adult
- playful
- inclusive
- sophisticated
- flavour-led
- light

## Rejected vibes
Reject anything that makes PEP feel like:
- a gym-bro protein shake
- a sports recovery drink
- a clinical supplement
- a productivity or biohacking drink
- a medical wellness product
- a kids’ soda or juice
- a heavy meal replacement
- a copied non-alcoholic spirit brand
- drinkpep.com or any energy/probiotic name-collision association

## Visual direction — strategy only
Design concept from the active positioning: **fun, premium packaging, vibrant, festival vibe, fruity**.

Interpret this as an identity that feels high-energy, flavour-forward, social, premium-but-approachable, and made for being seen in hand at a venue. The brand should have enough polish for hospitality, enough fun for festivals, and enough refreshment cues for a fruit-led protein drink.

Do:
- Lead with occasion and flavour before nutrition.
- Make the product feel orderable in bars and restaurants.
- Keep layouts confident, memorable, and not overly busy.
- Use packaging and web composition that can support multiple flavours as a lively range.
- Show serve moments, hands, friends, tables, movement, and outdoor social energy.
- Treat Coconut Mango as the live hero SKU while leaving room for five upcoming flavours.

Do not:
- Put protein claims into a gym-style hierarchy.
- Make the design feel like a supplement aisle.
- Over-index on wellness calmness or daily habit minimalism.
- Over-copy the non-alcoholic spirits category, which can become too formal for PEP.
- Make the brand feel childish, chaotic, or cheap.

## Copy direction
PEP copy should sound like the friend who knows where the fun is, not the coach counting your macros.

Do:
- Speak in short, confident, social lines.
- Make the benefit feel emotional: better nights, lighter choices, more fun.
- Use protein as a clever bonus: “with protein” rather than “for protein goals.”
- Keep the tagline visible: **Feel good. Have fun.**
- Emphasise taste, refreshment, flavour, and the social ritual.

Do not:
- Use fitness transformation language.
- Lead with grams, calories, targets, recovery, or discipline.
- Sound medicinal, moralising, or restrictive.
- Make alcohol the enemy; position PEP as a more exciting option, not a lecture.

## Competitor split

### In-line brands: strict fit
These brands match the active social direction and the drinking context closely enough to guide PEP’s brand world:
- Kin Euphorics — social, mood-led, experiential non-alcoholic drinking.
- Lyre’s — alcohol-free social ritual and cocktail replacement.
- Seedlip — premium non-alcoholic social drinking.
- Royal Club Mocktails — adult, flavour-led, non-alcoholic social serve.
- Royal Club Ginger Ale — adult mixer and social soft drink reference.

### Positioning fit but drink-type mismatch
These brands are useful inspiration, but PEP must not become them:
- Rekorderlig — strong fruit-led social occasion cues, but alcoholic cider is not PEP’s format.
- On The Rocks Cocktails — premium bar-style RTD cues, but alcoholic cocktail is not PEP’s format.
- Ole cocktails — celebratory RTD energy and flavour-led convenience, but partly alcoholic and originally mapped to lifestyle.

Borrow their occasion confidence, serve culture, hospitality fit, and flavour hierarchy. Do not borrow alcohol dependence or category conventions too literally.

### Other positioning peers
Functional and lifestyle brands may be useful for market awareness, but they are not PEP’s selected direction. They usually lead with gym utility, hydration, wellness routine, gut health, or everyday lifestyle upgrade. PEP must lead with social desire.

## Mechanical brand-fit rules
1. If the idea would make someone say “I order this when I go out,” it fits.
2. If the idea would make someone say “I use this to hit my protein target,” it belongs to direction 1, not this brand.
3. Lead with taste, fun, and occasion; mention protein as a bonus.
4. Hospitality and event contexts come before gym, desk, and routine contexts.
5. The brand should feel adult and premium, never clinical or bro-ish.
6. Fruit cues are welcome when they support flavour and refreshment, not when they make PEP feel childish.
7. Avoid copying alcohol brands; borrow the ritual, not the category costume.
8. Never confuse PEP at pep-drink.com with drinkpep.com.
