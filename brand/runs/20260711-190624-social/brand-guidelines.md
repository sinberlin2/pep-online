# PEP Brand Guidelines — Better social drink

## Direction
**Selected positioning:** 3 — Better social drink  
**Domain:** pep-drink.com  
**Founders:** Lou & Shannon  
**Tagline:** Feel good. Have fun.

PEP is a premium protein drink designed for social, out-of-home moments: bars, restaurants, hotels, festivals, dates, dinners, rooftops and group occasions. It should feel like something you choose because the moment deserves a better drink — not because you are tracking macros.

## One-line positioning
PEP is the light, refreshing protein drink for people who want to feel good and have fun when they go out.

## Who PEP is for
- Social drinkers who want something more exciting than a soft drink.
- People reducing or avoiding alcohol without wanting the evening to feel compromised.
- Taste-led, aesthetic customers who care how the drink feels in-hand and at the table.
- Hospitality customers who want a premium non-alcoholic option with a meaningful benefit.
- People who like wellness, but do not want a wellness lecture during a night out.

## When they drink it
- Ordering at a bar instead of beer, wine, cocktails or a basic soft drink.
- At dinner when they want a drink that still feels adult and intentional.
- At festivals, rooftops, hotels and events where the drink is part of the outfit, table and experience.
- On dates or group nights when they want to stay light, refreshed and socially present.
- As a premium, fruit-led social serve where protein is a pleasant bonus.

## Product role
PEP is a **social experience first** and a protein drink second. The protein benefit matters, but it should not dominate the brand story. In this direction, taste, occasion, ritual, flavour and premium feel are the buying drivers.

Live SKU: Coconut Mango. Five flavours are coming soon.

## Approved personality adjectives
Use: social, experiential, playful, premium, refreshing, fruity, sophisticated, adult, inclusive, euphoric, balanced, exciting, aesthetic, celebratory, light, confident.

## Rejected vibes
Avoid: gym-bro, clinical, supplement-like, medicinal, overly rational, meal-replacement, energy-drink aggressive, hard-party, childish, generic wellness, detox-coded, performance-obsessed.

## Visual strategy do’s
- Make PEP feel orderable in a bar, restaurant or event setting.
- Build around flavour, pleasure, serve ritual and social energy.
- Keep the design premium but not stiff; playful but not childish.
- Use spacious, confident information hierarchy so the pack feels hospitality-ready.
- Let protein appear as a smart supporting benefit, not the hero claim.
- Show the drink in groups, hands, dinner tables, bar moments, outdoor events and celebratory settings.
- Treat fruit and refreshment as sensory cues, not health-food decoration.

## Visual strategy don’ts
- Do not make the pack look like a sports supplement or gym fridge product.
- Do not lead with macros, training, recovery or optimization.
- Do not make PEP feel like a sober medicine, detox drink or functional tonic.
- Do not copy competitor logos, label systems, characters, bottle shapes or trade dress.
- Do not borrow alcohol cues so heavily that people think PEP contains alcohol.
- Do not make the brand too quiet, restrained or wellness-neutral; this direction needs social energy.

## Copy do’s
- Lead with the social moment: “when you’re out”, “for the table”, “your better round”, “feel good, have fun”.
- Make taste sound high-pleasure and refreshing.
- Use protein as a surprising benefit: light protein, feel-good protein, a better drink with 7g protein.
- Keep language short, confident and hospitality-friendly.
- Make it easy for venues to understand: a premium non-alcoholic social drink with protein.

## Copy don’ts
- Do not sound like a supplement label.
- Do not over-explain nutrition.
- Do not use gym language as the main hook.
- Do not moralize alcohol or shame drinking.
- Do not overuse wellness claims that make the product feel medicinal.

## Competitive reference split

### In-line brands — strict fit for positioning and social drink role
These are the closest strategic peers because they live in premium, adult, non-alcoholic or functional social drinking.

- Kin Euphorics — social, mood-led, experiential, functional without feeling like a supplement.
- Lyre’s — premium alcohol-free ritual and sophisticated serve culture.
- Seedlip — high-end non-alcoholic replacement for social occasions.
- Royal Club Mocktails — adult non-alcoholic mocktail alternative with clear social usage.
- Royal Club Ginger Ale — adult mixer logic and established out-of-home relevance.

### Positioning fit, type mismatch — use as design inspiration only
These brands nail parts of the social occasion, but their drink type is not PEP’s exact lane.

- Rekorderlig — fruit-forward premium cider; useful for social refreshment and festival energy, but alcoholic.
- On The Rocks Cocktails — bar-quality RTD cocktail cues and ready-to-serve confidence, but alcoholic.
- Ole cocktails — celebratory RTD energy and flavour-led hierarchy, but partly alcoholic and more lifestyle-party than protein-led.
- Yaya Kombucha — premium craft refreshment and natural flavour credibility, but kombucha is more wellness/craft than bar-order ritual.
- Walker Brothers — craft complexity and alternative-to-alcohol mindset, but kombucha/botanical soda is not PEP’s core format.

### Other positionings — useful context, not the active PEP direction
Functional protein and lifestyle/wellness brands should not define this direction. They may be useful category context, but PEP should not become a gym tool or everyday wellness soda in this route.

## Mechanical brand fit rules
1. If the idea makes PEP easier to order at a bar, dinner, hotel or festival, it likely fits.
2. If the idea makes PEP look like a gym protein drink, reject it.
3. Lead with flavour, pleasure and occasion before nutrition.
4. Protein is a feel-good benefit, not the core social promise.
5. The brand should feel premium enough for hospitality, but relaxed enough for friends.
6. Alcoholic RTDs can inspire serve ritual and occasion energy, but never imply PEP is alcoholic.
7. Avoid any confusion with drinkpep.com; PEP is Lou & Shannon’s premium protein drink at pep-drink.com.
