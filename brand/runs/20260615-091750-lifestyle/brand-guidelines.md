# PEP Brand Guidelines — Lifestyle Upgrade

Prepared for Lou & Shannon for **pep-drink.com**.

## Active positioning

**Direction:** Lifestyle upgrade  
**Tagline:** Feel good. Have fun.  
**Positioning statement:** PEP is a premium, light, refreshing protein drink for wellness-minded people who want healthier everyday choices to feel easy, tasty, and socially natural — not like a workout task.

PEP should make the consumer think: **“This fits my lifestyle.”**

## What PEP is

PEP is a premium protein drink that sits in everyday lifestyle moments: brunch cafés, coworking breaks, beach days, afternoon refreshment, wellness retail, and casual out-of-home occasions.

It is not a gym shake, not an energy drink, not a meal replacement, and not the US product at drinkpep.com.

Known product facts:

- Brand: PEP
- Domain: pep-drink.com
- Founders: Lou & Shannon
- Tagline: Feel good. Have fun.
- Live SKU: Coconut Mango
- Five flavours coming soon
- Protein: 7g
- Calories: 49
- Gluten-free

## Who PEP is for

PEP is for people who care about wellness and self-image, but do not want health choices to feel strict, technical, or joyless.

Primary audience:

- Wellness-conscious café and coworking consumers
- People who want a refreshing treat with a better nutritional profile
- People who buy drinks partly because the brand reflects their identity
- Light health optimisers who prefer ease over discipline
- Social/out-of-home consumers who want a drink that feels elevated but relaxed
- People replacing sugary soft drinks, heavy smoothies, snacks, or routine café drinks

## When they drink it

PEP belongs in lifestyle moments where taste, refreshment, and brand feel matter more than hardcore nutrition claims:

- Brunch or late-morning café orders
- Coworking breaks and desk-side refreshment
- Afternoon pick-me-ups without an energy-drink feel
- Beach, park, and casual outdoor moments
- Wellness retail trips
- Light social moments with friends
- On-the-go snack replacement when a full meal feels too heavy

PEP should feel visible and shareable, but not nightlife-led. It can be social without becoming a bar-first mocktail brand.

## Core brand idea

**Wellness without sacrifice.**

PEP’s protein benefit should be present and credible, but it should not dominate the brand. The lifestyle promise comes first: refreshing, tasty, feel-good, effortless.

Protein is the smart reason to believe. Taste and lifestyle are the reason to choose.

## Copy strategy

### Voice

PEP copy should be friendly, light, confident, and easygoing. It should feel like a stylish friend recommending a better drink — not a coach, doctor, or nutritionist.

### Copy should do

- Lead with taste, refreshment, and feel-good moments
- Make protein feel effortless and incidental, not intense
- Keep claims simple and human
- Use short, memorable phrases
- Tie PEP to real lifestyle occasions
- Sound optimistic and relaxed
- Keep the tagline close to the core brand idea: **Feel good. Have fun.**

### Copy should not do

- Over-explain nutrition
- Sound like sports performance marketing
- Use guilt, discipline, or punishment language
- Promise transformation or extreme results
- Make PEP feel like medicine
- Over-index on sobriety or nightlife rituals
- Confuse PEP with drinkpep.com

### Words and phrases to avoid

Avoid: gains, shred, macros-first, no excuses, crush it, elite performance, anabolic, bulk, cut, detox, cleanse, fat-burning, guilt-free, cheat day, meal replacement, biohack, clinical-grade, prescribed, sober ritual, cocktail replacement.

## Visual strategy

This is strategy only. Do not treat this as a finished identity system.

### Desired feel

PEP should feel airy, relaxed, premium-but-approachable, refreshing, unforced, and lifestyle-led. The visual world should communicate healthy pleasure rather than functional discipline.

### Design concept

Use the active Lifestyle upgrade direction: **more minimal and gentle, with spacious composition, low clutter, soft energy, and a refined everyday feel.**

The design should make PEP feel like a lifestyle accessory or daily habit. It should be clear enough to sell the protein benefit, but not so technical that it becomes a supplement.

### Visual do

- Use spacious layouts with a clear hierarchy
- Keep the product benefit easy to understand
- Create a calm, elevated lifestyle feel
- Show real everyday occasions: brunch, coworking, beach, afternoon refreshment
- Let flavour feel appetising and refreshing
- Keep the brand confident but not loud
- Make room for the product to feel premium and casual at the same time

### Visual don’t

- Do not use dense supplement-style information layouts
- Do not imitate gym RTD or sports nutrition packaging
- Do not make the brand feel medicinal, technical, or clinical
- Do not make it look like an energy drink
- Do not make it childish or candy-like
- Do not over-style it into a nightlife or festival brand
- Do not copy competitor logos, packaging systems, or distinctive assets

## Brand reference split

### In-line brands — strict fit

These are the closest strategic peers for the Lifestyle upgrade direction because they combine wellness, refreshment, taste, and identity-driven branding in everyday drink occasions.

- **BLOOM** — wellness soda precedent; fun, refreshing, gut-health-led, lifestyle-friendly.
- **OLIPOP** — healthier soda replacement with strong taste and wellness credibility.
- **Poppi** — modern, trend-aware prebiotic soda with strong lifestyle adoption.
- **Alive & Well** — kombucha positioned around approachable wellness, softer taste, and refreshment.
- **Recess** — lifestyle drink built around calm, ease, and daily wellness moments.
- **more fiz** — functional soda peer; healthier refreshment with everyday drink logic.
- **Yaya Kombucha** — strong kombucha benchmark for premium craft, authenticity, and healthy refreshment.
- **Walker Brothers** — craft kombucha/botanical benchmark for nuanced, natural, adult refreshment.

### Positioning fit, type mismatch

These brands have useful lifestyle cues, but their core format is less directly comparable to PEP.

- **EVER TREE / Everleaf** — useful for refined natural refreshment and premium soft-drink cues, but more aperitif-led than everyday protein refreshment.
- **Ole cocktails** — useful for convenient, premium, social lifestyle energy, but the cocktail/mocktail frame is not PEP’s core.
- **Ish** — useful for modern premium alcohol-free lifestyle cues, but too close to spirits/mocktails to be a direct model.

### Peer brands from other positionings

These are relevant for contrast, but should not define PEP’s Lifestyle upgrade direction.

- **HOLY** — too functional and supplement-led.
- **LMNT** — too hydration/electrolyte and performance-rational.
- **NOCCO** — too energy/sports-led.
- **PROTOCHOL** — protein plus alcohol novelty; wrong association for PEP.
- **VITAMIN WELL** — useful hydration benchmark but too functional as a primary direction.
- **Kin Euphorics** — social and mood-led; too close to the Better social drink territory.
- **Lyre’s** — non-alcoholic spirit framing; too cocktail and ritual-led.
- **Seedlip** — sophisticated social substitute; too spirits-led and not everyday wellness enough.

## Mechanical brand fit rules

Another agent should apply these rules when generating strategy, copy, page structure, or creative concepts:

1. If the idea feels like gym protein, reject it.
2. If the idea makes protein the whole story, reduce it; PEP is lifestyle-first, protein-supported.
3. If the idea feels refreshing, tasty, light, and socially easy, it likely fits.
4. If the moment is brunch, coworking, beach, wellness retail, or afternoon refreshment, it fits.
5. If the moment is mainly gym recovery, athletic performance, or macro tracking, it belongs to direction 1, not PEP’s active direction.
6. If the moment is mainly bars, festivals, nightlife, or cocktail replacement, it belongs to direction 3, not PEP’s active direction.
7. If the design is dense, technical, aggressive, or supplement-like, reject it.
8. If the design is spacious, calm, premium-but-approachable, and lifestyle-led, it fits.
9. If copy uses guilt, discipline, transformation, or extreme health claims, reject it.
10. Always distinguish PEP at pep-drink.com from drinkpep.com.

## Final north star

PEP should feel like the drink people choose when they want to make a better choice without making a big deal out of it.

**Feel good. Have fun.**
