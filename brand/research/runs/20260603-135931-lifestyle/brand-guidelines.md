# PEP — Brand Guidelines: Lifestyle Upgrade Positioning

**For:** Lou & Shannon @ pep-drink.com  
**Direction:** Lifestyle upgrade (wellness-without-sacrifice; feel-good, social, light)

---

## Who PEP Is For / When They Drink It

PEP is made for wellness-minded adults who want their healthy choices to feel like a treat. It's the perfect fit for:
- Urban professionals, creatives, and social wellness lovers
- People who see healthy eating/drinking as part of their identity, but want it effortless
- Anyone seeking a refreshing boost with real benefits — minus the "intense" gym or diet fad associations

**When?**
- Brunch with friends (think: not heavy, bright café vibes)
- Afternoon at a coworking space
- Beach days, picnic moments, post-yoga treat
- Any ordinary day where a light, healthy refreshment with a touch of fun feels right

---

## Visual & Copy Dos and Don’ts

### Visual Do’s
- Use a minimal, pastel palette (cream, peach, orangeLight, subtle green, yellow from brand colors)
- Typographic structure: Didot (logo/premium), Montserrat (info), script (gently, e.g. tagline)
- Photography: Soft, light-filled, lifestyle moments (café, beach, desk, natural leisure)
- Gentle, friendly iconography for nutrition badges

### Visual Don’ts
- No sports/energy drink motifs (bold gradients, metallics, cartoon "power" figures)
- No stark, functional product layouts (bar graphs, "scientific" cues, or pill icons)
- Avoid festival/party packs, glitter/iridescent themes
- No gym, heavy athletics, or hardcore functional coding

### Copy Do’s
- Invite participation (“Perfect for brunch...” or “Your anytime boost”)
- Focus on feeling: balance, enjoyment, light wellness
- Keep it light, positive (“Refreshing protein, just for you.”)

### Copy Don’ts
- Never push performance (“Fuels your workout” or “muscle-building”)
- Don’t talk about restriction (“guilt-free” or “diet”)
- Avoid extreme/medical claims (“Zero sugar!” “Ultimate boost!” “Doctor-recommended”)
- No direct references to drinkpep.com (different product and market)

## Fitting Brands (Peers / Inspiration)

- **OLIPOP** — Soft, cheerful, modern wellness for all

### Anti-References (No-Gos)
- **Lean Body/gym RTD, NOCCO, LMNT** — Too functional, intense, or performance/gym-rooted; doesn't align with approachable wellness or daily lifestyle.
- **drinkpep.com** — Different product/naming/brand collision; do not reference.
- **Energy drink cues** — No harsh or "boost"-first branding.
- **Social mocktail-only brands (Kin Euphorics, Lyre’s, Seedlip)** — Too event-centric, night-out, less about balanced daily wellness

---

## Brand Fit Rules (How To Judge Fit)
- Must use pastel/cream-based, minimal color approach (from brand.json)
- No bold, sporty, gym, or clinical product tropes
- Voice: Always inviting, warm, light — never extreme or aggressive
- Typography: Didot for premium, Montserrat for clarity, script (Brittany Signature) softly for taglines only
- All copy and visuals should evoke balance, enjoyment, and effortless wellness
- Peer references limited to lifestyle wellness drinks (not gym, not night-out)
- Never reference or resemble drinkpep.com
- Always reinforce the idea: PEP is a modern lifestyle treat, not a gym tool or party drink
