# PEP Brand Guidelines — Functional Protein Drink Positioning

## Who PEP is for — and when they drink it
- **Target audience:**
  - Fitness-minded professionals
  - Urbanites with strict routines 
  - Recreational athletes and gym goers who *don't* buy into macho or faddy fitness culture
  - People optimizing nutrition, hydration, and health with purpose — not for show
  
- **Occasions:**
  - After gym, pilates, yoga
  - Post-run recovery
  - At work, mid-morning or afternoon
  - On the go between meetings, or between meals
  - Alone or in functional, calm settings — *not* parties or brunch socials

## Approved adjectives (brand feels…)
- Functional
- Light
- Clean
- Hydrating
- Efficient
- Disciplined
- Vitamin-enriched
- Health-focused
- Trustworthy

## Rejected vibes (never use or appear like…)
- Aggressively 'gym-bro' (Leaning into bodybuilder, hyper-masculine, "gains", or 'jacked')
- Cocktail/mocktail, spirit-alternative, party/nightlife cues
- Playful, juvenile, or wellness-treat posture (pastels, smile-face, cartoon)
- Relaxation/lifestyle luxury or beachy soft drink vibes
- Anything that looks like a kids’ drink, sugary soda, or new-age elixir

## Visual guidelines
### Do
- Use clean, functional layouts **(see: HOLY, LMNT, VITAMIN WELL)**
- Highlight product stats (protein per can, calories, key vitamins) clearly
- Emphasize a sense of performance, everyday health optimization, and clarity
- Color palette: Use only the approved palette from `brand.json` ([see color hexes in source]) — deep green, crisp cream, orange, and light yellow for 'bright health'.
- Typography:
  - Didot for logo/wordmark
  - Montserrat for numbers, badges, and body text
  - Brittany Signature **only** for tagline "Feel good. Have fun." — never for big headers
- Photography: Solo, active, or everyday settings. Bright, minimal, modern; no gym machinery; never nightclub, bar, or group party scenes.

### Don’t
- Use heavy, black+red color schemes, or 'muscle' imagery
- Add cocktails, spirits, or mocktail cues (no glasses, garnish, metallic gradients)
- Overdo script or playful/lifestyle fonts
- Allow design to mimic wellness "treat" products (pastels, soft blurs, cartoonish touches)
- Use prose like 'probiotic', 'detox', 'spirit', 'treat', or 'party'
- Reference drinkpep.com or copy their US energy/probiotic style

## Copy/Voice guidelines
### Do
- Use direct, rational, and optimistic statements: "Light protein. Easy recovery. Real results."
- Focus on functional benefit and how PEP makes routines more effective
- Reinforce convenience, efficiency, and clarity
- Tagline (fixed): **Feel good. Have fun.**

### Don’t
- Use slang or bro/fitness lingo (no "gains", "shredded", "swole", etc.)
- Suggest PEP is a treat, a reward, or a party drink
- Use puns, overpromise, or reference non-functional wellness trends

## Fitting brands (peer references)
- **HOLY**: Clean functional vitamin drink (for design and rational tone)
- **LMNT**: Straightforward, functional hydration (for info layout)
- **NOCCO**: Bold but clear sport recovery (for ingredient focus)
- **VITAMIN WELL**: Functional vitamin hydration (for white/clean labeling and stat callouts)

## Anti-references (avoid)
- Gym-bro RTD protein (Lean Body, Muscle Milk, etc.) — too aggressive/specialized
- drinkpep.com (different name, different market)
- Kids’ drinks/energy sodas
- Social/mocktail brands (Kin Euphorics, Lyre's, Seedlip)
- Wellness-prebiotic sodas (OLIPOP, POPP) — too playful and lifestyle

## Brand fit rules (mechanical check)
1. **Design must read functional/health-oriented at a glance; avoid lifestyle/party cues.**
2. **No cocktail/mocktail references—no alcohol messaging, no fruit garnish art, no metallics.**
3. **Colors: stick to cream, green, peach, orange, yellow from brand.json; avoid pastels or neon.**
4. **Voice: direct, clear, rational, positive—never jokey or hyperbolic.**
5. **No bro language, gym stereotypes, or highly gendered cues.**
6. **Photography: always clean, healthy, and individual-focused, not group/party/social.**
7. **Typography: Didot, Montserrat, Brittany Signature for tagline only.**
8. **Never refer to or pull elements from drinkpep.com or gym RTD protein brands.**
