# PEP Lifestyle Upgrade — Brand Guidelines

## Who is PEP for? When do they drink it?
PEP is crafted for wellness-oriented, design-conscious adults who want light, healthy refreshment that feels like a treat—not a supplement, and never a compromise. Our customers are urban professionals, creative freelancers, and health-forward young adults who ‘upgrade’ their day with better choices: at a sunlit café brunch, during a coworking afternoon, on the beach, or as a guilt-free snack swap at work. They value balance, delicious taste, and brands that make them feel seen, not marketed to.

## Approved Adjectives (Brand Personality)
- Refreshing
- Natural
- Subtle
- Vibrant
- Lively
- Soft
- Health-focused
- Prebiotic (or wellness-forward)
- Friendly

**Audience feels:** Stylish, positive, active (but not gym-obsessed), moderately premium, social but not party-focused. 

## Rejected Vibes / Anti-References
- **Gym-bro** or hypermasculine ‘protein’ branding 
- Energy drink codes (neon, metallic, busy, intense)
- Clinical or medicinal supplement design
- Party/festival-centric ‘hard seltzer’ or night-life looks
- Kids’ juice or cartoonish branding
- Anything resembling drinkpep.com (energy/probiotic, name overlap)

## Visual Guidelines: Do / Don’t
**Do:**
- Use a minimal, pastel, clear layout (ample white/cream/peach/yellow space)
- Soft type (Didot for logo, Montserrat/Sans for body, Brittany script for flair, per brand.json)
- Highlight protein and health, but as lifestyle “plus”—never in bold or gym/fitness traits
- Feature real-life, aspirational moments (café tables, sunlight, beaches, casual gatherings)
- Keep packaging and interface uncluttered, premium, but friendly—not ‘exclusive’
- Logo should feel light, approachable, and slightly playful

**Don’t:**
- Use sporty, bold, high-contrast ‘energy’ or supplement visuals
- Use words like ‘bulk’, ‘gains’, ‘performance’, or ‘maximize’
- Show people working out, running, or sweaty
- Resemble gym RTDs or hard seltzers
- Cram the layout; avoid black, metallic, or neon backgrounds
- Reference energy, intensity, or clinical/scientific benefit as the lead story

## Fitting Brands (References)
- **BLOOM:** Fun, lifestyle-forward, wellness positioning (prebiotic, vibrant)
- **EVER TREE:** Minimalist, elegant, ingredient-focused, and natural
- **OLIPOP:** Soft, wellness-savvy, approachable
- **POPP:** Lively, refreshing, everyday health occasions

## Anti-References (Brands to Avoid)
- Lean Body, NOCCO, or any gym-oriented ready-to-drink (RTD) protein — too performance/fitness
- drinkpep.com — name collision, different product and vibe
- Energy drinks, BCAA, or hard seltzer brands

## Brand Fit Rules (for easy application by future agents)
1. Reject any design, copy, or reference that is gym, sport, or overtly functional/protein-focused.
2. Approve only if the palette is soft/minimal, with cream, peach, pastel, and green/yellow/orange from brand.json.
3. Photography and visual narrative must show real, stylish, everyday occasions in relaxed, slightly aspirational settings.
4. Any copy should feel friendly, fresh, light, never clinical or intense.
5. Never use direct performance or supplement language, nor refer to energy or intense activity.
6. Always check references: if it ‘feels like a treat’ and could be in a café or beach cooler, it’s in. If it could be in a gym locker or club VIP table, it’s out.
