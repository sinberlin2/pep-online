# PEP Brand Guidelines — Lifestyle Upgrade Direction (Lou & Shannon)

## Who is PEP for and when do they drink it?
PEP is designed for wellness-focused city dwellers, young professionals, and anyone who wants a healthy boost that feels like a treat—not a chore or a gym routine. Drink PEP at brunches, during afternoon breaks at cafés or coworking spaces, after light exercise, on beach days, or as a refreshing daily ritual.

- **Audience:** Modern, health-conscious, style-aware, identity-driven consumers who want health to be easy, delicious, and aspirational.
- **Occasions:** Brunch, relaxed social moments, healthy treat alongside food, mid-afternoon pick-me-up, light wellness rituals.

## Approved adjectives (PEP should feel…)
- Healthy
- Refreshing
- Natural
- Probiotic
- Rejuvenating
- Light
- Modern
- Calm/Serene
- Approachable
- Aspirational (in an everyday, non-pretentious way)

## Rejected vibes (never look or sound like…)
- Gym-bro, macho, performance-maximizing
- Neon energy drink, "monster" mindset
- Medical, clinical, or supplement-focused
- Excessively techy, data-driven functional brands
- Event/festival/rave-focused aesthetics

## Visual and Copy Do/Don't

**DO:**
- Use pastel, natural, and serene color palettes
- Keep design uncluttered, minimal, light and friendly
- Let wordmarks and script add a warm, inviting touch (Didot + Brittany Signature as per brand)
- Feature sunlit, real-life photography in cafés, on blankets at the park, with brunch foods, or stylish accessories
- Use language that’s warm, joyful, and lightly aspirational—"Feel good. Have fun."

**DON’T:**
- Use jarring, bold, or "sports" visuals (no primary color blocking, gym icons, or performance gauges)
- Reference workout routines, "gains," "pump," or hardcore protein
- Over-promise on nutrition—PEP is about balanced daily wellness, not maximized performance
- Use heavy block fonts or clinical iconography
- Position the product as an energy drink or functional meal replacement

## Fitting Brands (References)
- **POPP:** Bright, pastel, clean designs; refreshing wellness tone
- **RITA:** Natural/probiotic messaging, earthy pastel style
- **AUNE & KEVAI:** Calm pastel branding and balanced, healthy recovery proposition

## Anti-References (Avoid)
- **Lean Body/gym RTD:** Too aggressive, gym-focused protein, hyper-masculine
- **drinkpep.com:** Name confusion; does not match visual or identity
- **NOCCO, PROSIP, VITAMIN WELL:** Functional/bold, fitness market—NOT for lifestyle/aspirational occasions
- **LYRE'S, Fishers Island, Recipes:** Social drink focus for nightlife/ritual—PEP is for healthy daily living

## Visual Style
- **Color palette:** Use brand JSON palette (see below); lean into cream, peach, yellow, green for warmth and calm.
- **Typography:** Elegant wordmark (Didot); body and stat details in Montserrat; uplifting touches with Brittany Signature (script), e.g. tagline/slogan. Avoid strict all-caps sans, avoid blockiness.
- **Photography:** Bright, candid, lightly styled real scenes—not sterile studio or staged.

## Tagline and Tone
- **Tagline:** Feel good. Have fun.
- **Tone:** Bright, light-hearted, stylish, never try-hard. Wellness as lifestyle, not discipline. Clean, warm copy—never clinical or aggressive.

## Brand Fit Rules (for mechanical application)
- Minimal, pastel, or earthy tones are required—NEVER neon or black-heavy.
- Copy must center on wellness, balance, refreshment, and "treat yourself"—NEVER on performance or athletic optimization.
- Use cases, visuals, and scenarios must be aspirational for daily wellness routines (e.g. café, coworking, park, not gym/festival/bar).
- Typography strictly from brand.json families—NO substitutes for core wordmark or script.
- NO references to protein-maximizing, "sports nutrition," or energy-boost gimmicks in copy.
