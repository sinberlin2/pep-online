# PEP Brand Guidelines — Better Social Drink Direction

## Who is PEP for? When do they drink it?
- **People:** Style-conscious, experience-driven adults (20s–40s) who love going out — but want something fresh, exciting, non-alcoholic, and not remotely boring.
- **Occasions:** Bars, dinners, music festivals, rooftop parties, date nights, celebrations, and social events where the drink is part of the vibe. PEP is never for the solo gym-goer or daily functional hydration.

---

## Approved Brand Adjectives
- Social
- Euphoric
- Premium
- Experience-driven
- Aesthetic
- Spirit-like
- Vibrant
- Fun
- Trendy

## Rejected Vibes (Never Use)
- Gym/fitness-bro, post-workout, sportsy
- Clinical/medical/functional-first
- Muted wellness minimalism or ‘cleansing’ looks
- Kid’s juice or anything childish
- Heavy, dark, or aggressive (energy drinks)
- Imitating drinkpep.com (US)

---

## Visual & Copy — Do / Don’t

### Visual DOs
- Use vibrant PEP brand colors (green #1f4d3a, orange #e86a1a, peach #fde8d8, yellow #fff4d6, white #ffffff, etc.)
- Feature lively, premium, festival-inspired settings (sunsets, neon, group toasts, glam party backdrops)
- Apply playful, bold typography: Didot (logo), Brittany Signature (script for taglines), Montserrat (labels/stat numbers), Bebas Neue (badges/highlights)
- Show product in chic bar glassware or at energetic group occasions

### Visual DON’Ts
- No gym, sports, or hydration bottle cues
- No clinical sterile backgrounds or supplement label layouts
- No heavy wellness or muted earth tones/minimalism
- Never childish or cartoon graphics
- No blacked-out energy drink aesthetic

### Copy/Voice DOs
- Make it feel like the start of the party: upbeat, inviting, fun, premium
- Reference social rituals, celebration, pleasure, and belonging
- Be playful — “Feel good. Have fun.” is always in the mix

### Copy/Voice DON’Ts
- No performance/gym claims (no ‘hit your macros’…)
- No wellness cliché language (‘cleanse’, ‘boost’, ‘restore health’)
- Don’t use supplement talk (no ‘hydrolyzed’, ‘BCAA’, ‘nutrition play’)

## Explicit Brand Fits (Peers)
**IN:**
- **Kin Euphorics** — Social, mood-driven, party-ready
- **Lyre’s** — Sophisticated, spirit-like, bar ritual
- **Seedlip** — Premium, non-alcoholic, built for social occasions

**OUT (Anti-references):**
- Lean Body / any gym protein RTD (functional, masculine, anti-social)
- drinkpep.com (different product, US market, and functional/energy focus, not PEP)
- NOCCO, LMNT (sports/clinical/hydration positioning)
- Wellness sodas or prebiotic drinks (bloom, olipop, poppi, ever tree… all more about wellness/health hacks, not night-out vibes)

---

## Brand Fit Rules (Directives)
1. **Always optimize for social, bar, and party first — never gym, daily wellness, or hydration.**
2. **Reject any design or copy with functional, clinical, or sports language.**
3. **Use premium, vibrant color and typography, avoiding minimal pastels or earthy naturalism.**
4. **Every brand or design touch-point should look and feel at home at a rooftop bar or music festival — never in a gym locker room or health food store.**
5. **Don’t reference or emulate drinkpep.com, or functional protein players.**
6. **Never let PEP be mistaken for a daily ‘healthy refreshment’—it’s always the life of the party, not a self-improvement tool.**
