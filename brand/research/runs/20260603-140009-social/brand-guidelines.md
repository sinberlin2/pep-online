# PEP Brand Guidelines — Better Social Drink Positioning

## Who PEP is for & When They Drink It

PEP is for social, outgoing adults (20s-40s) who want an exciting, premium alternative to alcohol in group settings. They love beautiful drinks, good company, and the shared rituals of going out—but don’t want to settle for soft drinks, sugary sodas, or feel pressured by booze.

**Drink PEP:**
- At bars, rooftop events, and cocktail lounges
- During dinners, dates, celebrations, and group occasions
- At music festivals, after-work socials, and any place where “holding a drink” feels like being part of the vibe

PEP fits wherever people want to join the fun, stand out, and feel part of the group—with a drink that’s as exciting as any cocktail, but alcohol-free (and with a little protein boost).

## Approved Adjectives  
**(All must fit)**
- Premium
- Social
- Vibrant
- Aesthetic
- Experiential
- Euphoric
- Trend-forward
- Alcohol-free

## Rejected Vibes/Inappropriate Associations
- “Gym-bro”, fitness or sports visuals
- Routine, optimization, or health-tracker energy
- Minimal pastel “lifestyle wellness” visuals
- Clinical, functional, or vitamin/supplemental design
- Overly masculine, harsh, or child-like/cutesy touches

## Visual & Copy DO/DON'T

### DO:
- Lean into vibrant, festival-inspired color pops—draw from PEP’s green, orange, cream, peach, yellow (see brand.json colors)
- Use elegant/modern typography and a mix of Didot (premium), Brittany Signature (script) and Montserrat (body, labels)
- Show inviting, lively group scenarios—dusk or evening light, celebratory/festive ambiance
- Use playful, energetic language that evokes belonging, celebration, and treat appeal
- Make packaging visually pop on the bar or table (bold, premium, fun)
- Keep copy inclusive, inviting, and experience/provenance focused (“Feels special”, “A toast without alcohol”)

### DON’T:
- Lead with protein content (“7g protein!”)—never the headline
- Show gym, running, or sports settings
- Use clinical/medical colors, “functional” charts, or sterile layouts
- Copy energy drink or gym drink can shape/branding
- Reference diets, routines, or optimization language (“fuel”, “macro”, “performance”)
- Mimic drinkpep.com (different product, risks confusion)

2. **Lyre's** — elegant, alcohol-free spirits for group occasions
3. **Seedlip** — premium, non-alc, cocktail ritual replacement

## Anti-References
1. **Lean Body / typical gym RTD** — fitness-first; performance, not pleasure
2. **drinkpep.com** — name collision and energy/probiotic, not social/protein
3. **NOCCO, LMNT, Vitamin Well** — functional, health-driven, not fun/group focused

## Layout-Only Inspirations
- **Revized** (for modular flavor grid or site structure only—never for tone or visuals)

## Brand Fit Rules
- If it feels at home at a festival bar, chic rooftop, or dinner party, it fits.
- If the vibe is “pre/post workout”, “daily health routine” or “vitamin shot”, it does NOT fit.
- Vibrant/energetic > muted/pastel or cold/clinical.
- Always broadcast fun, premium, inclusive, and “drinking something special together”.
- Never blend with energy drink, gym protein, or wellness minimalism tropes.
