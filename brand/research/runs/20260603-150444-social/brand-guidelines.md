# PEP Brand Guidelines — Social Direction (“Better Social Drink”)

## Who PEP is for, and when they drink it
PEP is made for social, urban adults—often between 20 and 40—who prioritize experience and aesthetics. They want to join in every moment: rooftop parties, music festivals, lively dinners, dates, or just when they’re out with friends. They don’t want to be left with a plain soda, and they’re looking for a drink that feels as fun and premium as the best cocktails—but without the alcohol.

## What PEP is (and isn’t)
- **What it is:** A light, sparkling, protein-based drink you can enjoy out—at a bar or with a group—instead of alcoholic options. Main SKU: Coconut Mango, with a vibrant, fruity, refreshing taste. Served cold in premium glass or can packaging.
- **What it’s not:** Not a gym protein shake, meal replacement, nor a wellness tonic for solo routines.

## Core personality & voice
### Approved adjectives (DO)
- Fun, social, euphoric, experience-driven, aesthetic, premium, vibrant, trendy, authentic, inclusive

### Rejected vibes (DON’T)
- Gym, clinical, functional, minimalist-pastel, medical, performance, kid’s juice, ‘wellness ritual for one’

### Visual/Craft
#### DO:
- Use vibrant, festival-inspired color pops (see brand palette)
- Emphasize group and event energy in visuals (smiling faces, glasses clinking, party tables)
- Design packaging for visibility in bars, parties, or dinner tables (bold type, contrast, flavor cues)
- Hero typography: Didot (logo), Brittany Signature (tagline), Montserrat (labels)
- Show the drink in rich color, glittering with condensation

#### DON’T:
- Use fitness/athletic motifs
- Lean into gym or functional beverage visual codes
- Feature sterile nutritional badges as hero
- Default to muted/clinical wellness soft-drink looks or ‘meditation’ stock
- Overdo script or playful fonts: stay premium
- Use soft pastels or clinical whites/blues as primary palette

#### Approved colors and fonts
- Colors: green (#1f4d3a), greenDark (#163a2c), orange (#e86a1a), orangeLight (#f4a261), cream (#faf6f0), peach (#fde8d8), yellow (#fff4d6), white (#ffffff)
- Fonts: Didot (logo), Brittany Signature (script/tagline), Montserrat (labels/statistics), Bebas Neue (badges)

## Explicit peer splits
### In-line brands (social ritual + social drink):
- Kin Euphorics — trendy, euphoric group energy & mood-enhancing non-alc drinks
- Lyre’s — elegant, cocktail-quality non-alc spirits for social experiences
- Seedlip — premium, ritual-forward, aesthetically beautiful non-alc spirit
- Ole Cocktails — colorful, modern, premium RTDs for vibrant group events

### Branding-fit, type mismatch (social energy but wrong format):
- Protochol — Social drink branding, but contains alcohol/protein fusion (not non-alc)

### Other-positioning peers (not for social lane):
- Brands with main focus on functional health/wellness, sports/fitness, or solo routines (HOLY, LMNT, NOCCO, Vitamin Well, Bloom, Olipop, Ever Tree, Poppi, Ish, Recess)
- Kombucha and wellness drinks unless they are clearly bar/event-first in visual & message

### Anti-references
- Lean Body/gym RTDs (protein-fitness world)
- drinkpep.com (US; unrelated product with same name)

## Brand fit rules (mechanical application)
- Accept brands ONLY if explicitly social, premium, and group-occasion in both message & look
- If product is gym or functional in core, reject or anti-reference
- If design leans clinical, pastel-minimalist, or ‘therapy drink’, reject
- Never copy from drinkpep.com or any gym-protein shake
