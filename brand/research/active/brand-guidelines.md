## PEP Brand Guidelines (Lifestyle Upgrade – Version 1.0)

---

### Who PEP is for, and when they drink it
- PEP is for modern, wellness-conscious adults who want to enjoy something refreshing, healthy, and social — no protein shakes, no gym-chugging, just light, fun, everyday pleasure with perks.
- Ideal for brunch with friends, afternoon coworking, sunny days at the park, laid-back yoga flow follow-ups, or simply an inspiring break from boring sodas.
- Main audience: urban adults (millennials and Gen Z), especially those who see their drink choice as part of their self-care and daily image. They're not chasing gym gains — they want to feel good, have fun, and make better choices without overthinking it.

### Approved adjectives
- Refreshing, vibrant, approachable, modern, balanced, smooth, lively, subtle, premium, natural, health-focused, authentic, playful, inclusive

### Rejected vibes/adjectives
- Clinical, sporty, gym-bro, medical, supplement, intense, hyperactive, muscle-building, techy/energy, 'bulking', childlike, bodybuilding, utilitarian, performance-focused

### Brand voice: Do/Don't
**DO:**
- Use warm, accessible, and uplifting language. Invite people to enjoy life, not optimize it. Lead with taste and enjoyment. Let health feel like a treat, not a task.
- Talk about moments ("for your next brunch break", "adds a little sunshine to your afternoon").
- Use copy that sounds like a friend recommending something great.

**DON'T:**
- Don't use fitness jargon (e.g., 'muscle', 'fuel', 'shred', 'macros', 'BCAA').
- Don't position as an energy drink, sports performance aid, or meal replacement.
- Don't invoke diet, restriction, or body transformation.
- Avoid mentioning 'workout', 'gains', 'sweat', or anything that feels exclusive or intimidating.

### Visual: Do/Don't
**DO:**
- Use minimal, pastel, and soft color palettes (see brand.json: #1f4d3a green, #e86a1a orange, #fde8d8 peach, #fff4d6 yellow, etc.).
- Photography: Airy, natural lighting; candid, happy, relaxed lifestyle scenes. Casual, aspirational community shots; brunches, coworking, sun, greenery.
- Layout: Clean, uncluttered. Elegant serif for the wordmark (Didot); crisp Montserrat for clarity. Brittany Signature for the script/tagline ("Feel good. Have fun.").
- Package cues: Real fruit, nature, or healthy vitality — not vitamins or macros.

**DON'T:**
- No shaker bottles, sports paraphernalia, protein stats as the hero.
- No clinical/medical cues or pharma-like labeling.
- No dark, neon, or metallic "energy" or "gym" colors.
- Never use busy, technical, or over-labelling.

### Explicit List – Fitting Brands (Benchmarks)
**In-line (look, feel, vibe):**
- BLOOM, EVER TREE, OLIPOP, Poppi, Alive & Well, Recess, Ole cocktails, Ish, Yaya Kombucha, Walker Brothers
- All use approachable, modern, soft design with a wellness/lifestyle focus.

**Anti-references (reject):**
- Lean Body/gym RTDs
- drinkpep.com (US probiotic/energy, name collision)
- Generic energy/protein drinks (NOCCO, LMNT, functional sports shakes)
- Any brand that feels tech-driven, intimidating, childlike, or hyperactive.

**Layout-only references:**
- You may use sites with modern, clear, modular layouts (e.g., Revized, Recess) for web structure only, but reject their copy/visual attitude if not lifestyle/aligned.

### Brand Fit Rules (for other agents, strict)
- Reject all brands/looks with heavy gym, energy, performance, or medical cues.
- Prioritize brands built for daily wellness, casual fun, leisure, and social self-image.
- Layouts must express airy, minimal, pastel themes — never clinical, never hyperactive.
- Accept only those that use approachable, friendly, inclusive copy and visuals.
- Do not reference or platform any 'drinkpep.com' materials.
- Never let protein or macros be the hero of the story — enjoyment and mood come first.
