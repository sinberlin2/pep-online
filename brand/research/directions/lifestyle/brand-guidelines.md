## PEP Brand Guidelines — Lifestyle Upgrade Direction (Lou & Shannon)

### Who is PEP for?
- **Young professionals & health-conscious urbanites** (20s/30s): people who want daily wellness with enjoyment, not restriction
- **Aspirational, social, stylish individuals**: people who care about self-care, culture, and community; brunch and outdoor moments, not just workouts
- Ideal for: remote workers, brunch-goers, yoga & wellness enthusiasts, conscious socializers

### When and where do they drink PEP?
- Morning or afternoon café sessions (in place of a classic soda or kombucha)
- Making coworking and creative sessions feel special
- Balancing a beach day, picnic, or stroll (something light, never heavy)
- After light exercise, yoga, or during a 'treat yourself' break
- Social gatherings or casual meetups with friends

### Approved adjectives & personality
- **Refreshing**, **light**, **modern**, **authentic**, **balanced**, **premium but approachable**, **playful**, **natural**, **inclusive**, **warm**
- PEP = friendly, uplifting, and subtly elevated. Never intimidating, clinical, or overly functional.

### Rejected vibes (anti-references)
- "Gym protein," "shred," "bulk," "energy boost," or anything aggressive/masculine
- Clinical/technical/medicinal branding (no supplement bottle, bold metallics, Rx copy)
- Kids’ soda, anything cartoonish or childish
- Night-life/bar/club neon influence (save festival for future innovation)
- DO NOT copy or draw inspiration from drinkpep.com (energy/probiotic US drink)

### Visual design — Do & Don’t
**DO:**
- **Minimal, pastel, and cream palette** — soft peach, cream, warm yellows, modern green accents (see brand.json)
- Real, inviting, daylight-focused photography: fresh, outdoors, communal, or mindful solo moments
- Typography: Didot (elegant serif for main wordmark/headline), Brittany Signature (warm script accents), Montserrat (clean sans for body & stats)
- Clear callout badges (protein, calories) using Montserrat or Bebas Neue. Subtle, not shouty.

**DON’T:**
- Use bold, high-saturation colors, metallics, or "energy drink" motifs
- Show gyms, weights, working out, or 'performance' scenes
- Overcrowd with functional claims — wellness, not performance
- Use childish or garish layouts
- Emulate competitor drinkpep.com, nor any mass-market sports protein

### Copy voice — Do & Don’t
**DO:**
- Friendly, uplifting, inclusive — "This fits my lifestyle," not "achieve your goals"
- Tone: modern, subtly witty, relaxed confidence
- Tagline: "Feel good. Have fun."

**DON’T:**
- Use restriction, guilt, or optimization language
- Over-index on technical detail (grams of BCAAs, etc.)
- Rely on gym jargon, bro talk, or fitness clichés

### Peer Brands: Clear SPLIT
- **inLineBrands:** Brands like Olipop, Recess, Alive & Well, Bloom, Ever Tree, Poppi, Ole Cocktails, Ish — all benchmark the modern, minimal, lifestyle-upgrade role for PEP
- **positioningFitTypeMismatch:** Authentic kombuchas (Yaya, Walker Brothers) — great wellness/lifestyle vibe, but not close on drink format
- **peerBrandsOtherPositionings:** LMNT, NOCCO, Vitamin Well, HOLY (hydration/gym/functional lane); Kin Euphorics, Lyre’s, Seedlip (alcohol substitute, social event focus)

### Brand Fit Rules (for teams/partners)
- Always uphold a refreshing, modern, aspirational wellness mood
- Never allow gym, performance, or clinical cues in main design/copy
- Use approved fonts and color palette; avoid copycat or generic wellness layouts
- Celebrate lifestyle occasions: brunch, beach, coworking — YES; gym, bar, or club — NO
- Keep messaging focused on joy, lightness, and self-image, not discipline or maximum function
