# PEP Site Concept — Whole-Site Layout & Brand Guidance (June 2024)

**Summary for Lou & Shannon:**

PEP’s new web vision is light, premium, and welcoming to all — not just gym-goers. The site calls out the protein + refreshment advantage, heroing flavour, occasions, and your own social story. We use the trusted greens, creams, and pastel highlights, never veering toward seltzer-club neon or gym-shake chrome.

**Navigation:** Simple DTC logic. For consumers: Drinks/Flavours, Enjoy/Occasions, About, For Venues. For venues: clear sales/proof angle, fast pilot signup form, and social proof (future growth).

---

## index.html — Consumer Page

**Hero**: Split (left: copy/claims, right: can and glass, soft citrus, good light). Above-the-fold key claims:
- *Light. Refreshing. Protein boost.*
- Nutrient claims bar (7g protein, 49 cal, no sugar, gluten-free)
- "Explore flavours" CTA

**Drinks**: Flavour-forward grid or scroll (one live, others 'coming soon'). Colour backgrounds per SKU. Each card: large can visual, brief sensory copy, 'Vote for next' on unlaunched SKUs.

**Enjoy**: Occasion cards (icons + headline): Café mornings, terrace, post-workout, socials. 4–6 modish blocks — not lists. Each hints at the real-life, not gym-floor, PEP moments.

**About**: Lou & Shannon’s founder story: photo plus copy, left/right. Script tagline: “Feel good. Have fun.”

**Venue CTA Band**: Full-width, cream or soft green. One-liner and button to For Venues.

**Footer:** Script line, leaf dot. Optional: email/newsletter signup as site grows.

---

## for-venues.html — Venues Page

**Hero**: Direct, business-aware. Value prop for serving PEP: stand out, boost guest delight, modern health positioning. Bar/café product-in-situ image. CTA: Join pilot (scrolls form).

**Benefits**: 3–5 icon cards—margin, uniqueness, easy menu add, fits all-day. Headline, icon, short copy.

**Pilot How-to**: Stepwise illustration—get samples, serve, gather guest reactions, feedback (linear, friendly, not dense).

**Form**: Clean, vertical panel. Venue name, contact, role, size/volume, short note. Brand-colour button.

**Testimonials** (future): Carousel or grid with quote, venue logo/face, or guest quote. Light placeholder if none yet.

**Footer/Link-back**: Soft CTA to return to consumer site, logo home-link.

## Brand Visual & UX Rules
- **Colors/fonts:** Always from brand.json — greens, creams, peaches, yellow; Didot, Bebas Neue, Montserrat, Brittany/Segoe Script.
- **Layout:** Airy, pleasant, section blocks with soft palette transitions; grid for occasion/flavour lists, not full-bleed walls.
- **Copy:** Warm, inviting, never gym-bro or energy drink style.
- **Icons:** Adapt pattern, draw your own or use outlines/line icons. Never lift asset files directly.

---

## Key Patterns to Steal/Adapt
- Hero split with claims (Revized)
- Flavour grid/scroll (Revized, drinkpep layout)
- Occasions/serving grid (Revized, Lumio)
- Benefit bar (drinkpep, re-cast for nutrition)
- Founder/about (Revized, current PEP)
- Venue inquiry flow/button (Revized, best of DTC pilots)

## Key Patterns to Avoid
- Neon club/seltzer or grey/black gym motifs
- Childish/cartoon elements
- Technical macro/micro breakdowns (focus on practical benefit, not nutrient science)
- Any copy from drinkpep.com (label all with 'name collision: US energy brand, layout ref only')

---

## Flavour Cards Guidance
- One live (Coconut Mango): large, prominent, 'Available now' badge
- Others (Pineapple Lime, etc): subdued 'Coming soon' cards, encourage votes—no hard sell or annoying lock icons
- Soft, ingredient-inspired backgrounds (use brand tints)

---

## Form Experience (Venues)
- Gentle onboarding (no overwhelming sales lingo)
- Form sections lightly marked, generous spacing
- Success state confirms form receipt and offers return path to DTC
